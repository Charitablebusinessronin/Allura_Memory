# [1] Name Rationale

## Why "allura" Was Chosen

After extensive exploration — including brainstorming sessions, linguistic analysis, and careful consideration of our brand DNA (Warm + Connected) — we selected **allura** as our brand name.

### The Decision Criteria

| Criterion | Weight | allura Score |
|-----------|--------|--------------|
| **Warmth** | High | A+ — The soft sounds and flowing syllables evoke comfort and approachability |
| **Memorability** | High | A — Short, distinctive, easy to recall and spell |
| **Uniqueness** | Medium | A- — Stands out in the tech space without being unpronounceable |
| **Domain Availability** | High | B+ — allura.io available; .com acquired |
| **Cultural Resonance** | Medium | A — Appeals across urban cultures; feels inclusive |
| **Scalability** | Medium | A — Works as a platform name, product family, or verb |
| **Emotional Pull** | High | A — Suggests attraction, invitation, magnetism |

### The Core Reasoning

**allura** embodies our mission in a single word: *to draw people together.*

The name suggests **allure** — that magnetic quality that draws people in — but softened, made more approachable. We dropped the final 'e' to create something unique and ownable while maintaining the phonetic warmth.

Consider the associations:
- **Allure** — attraction, charm, invitation
- **Aura** — the energy field around something alive
- **All** — inclusivity, everyone belongs
- **You** — the individual at the center

Combined: *the magnetic energy that draws everyone together, with you at the heart of it.*

That's allura.

# [2] Name Analysis

## Linguistic Breakdown

### Phonetic Structure

**allura** — /əˈlʊərə/ or /ah-LOOR-ah/

| Syllable | Sound | Quality |
|----------|-------|---------|
| al- | Soft "ah" + liquid "l" | Gentle opening, inviting |
| -lu- | "loo" with rounded lips | Warm, contained, comfortable |
| -ra | Open "ah" ending | Expansive, welcoming finish |

### Etymological Roots

**Primary root:** English "allure" (from Old French *aleurrer*, "to attract")
- *a-* (to) + *leurre* (bait, lure)
- Originally meant "to entice with promise"

**Secondary resonance:** Latin "aura" (breath, breeze, atmosphere)
- Suggests something intangible but felt
- Implies life, movement, presence

**Tertiary resonance:** Spanish/Italian "allora" (then, so, well then)
- Conversational transition word
- Implies continuity, connection, flow

### Phonoaesthetic Qualities

**Soft consonants:** The name contains no hard stops (p, t, k, b, d, g). The liquid 'l' and soft 'r' create a flowing, continuous sound — like conversation, like community.

**Vowel harmony:** Three open vowels (a-u-a) create a warm, resonant tone. No sharp edges. No harsh sounds.

**Rhythm:** Three syllables with stress on the middle — da-DUM-da. This mirrors the rhythm of invitation: *come INside*.

### Connotations Map

```
allura
├── Warmth (primary)
│   └── Inviting, nurturing, comfortable
├── Magnetism (primary)
│   └── Draws people in, creates attraction
├── Community (secondary)
│   └── All + You + Us = Together
├── Lightness (secondary)
│   └── Airy, breathable, unburdened
└── Feminine Energy (secondary)
    └── Nurturing, intuitive, relational
```

# [3] Naming Alternatives Considered

## The Shortlist

During our naming process, we evaluated over 50 candidates. Here are the finalists and why they didn't make the cut:

### Option 1: **Gatherly**

**Why we considered it:**
- Clear community focus
- Friendly, approachable
- Verb potential ("Let's gatherly")

**Why we passed:**
- Too descriptive; lacks mystery
- Sounds like a feature, not a platform
- Domain availability poor
- Feels temporary, not timeless

**Verdict:** Rejected — Too literal, not evocative enough.

---

### Option 2: **Kinde**

**Why we considered it:**
- Speaks to kindness, care
- Short, punchy
- Warm emotional core

**Why we passed:**
- "Kind" is overused in tech
- Sounds like "kinda" — weakens positioning
- Limited scalability beyond social good

**Verdict:** Rejected — Too narrow, lacks aspiration.

---

### Option 3: **Voxly**

**Why we considered it:**
- Voice-focused (vox = voice in Latin)
- Short, modern
- Good domain availability

**Why we passed:**
- Too focused on expression, not connection
- Sounds slightly clinical
- Misses the warmth component

**Verdict:** Rejected — Too sharp, not warm enough.

---

### Option 4: **Communa**

**Why we considered it:**
- Direct community reference
- Feminine ending adds warmth
- Established Latin root

**Why we passed:**
- Sounds like a government program
- A bit heavy, institutional
- Doesn't suggest magnetism or attraction

**Verdict:** Rejected — Too institutional, not inviting.

---

### Option 5: **Nestwell**

**Why we considered it:**
- Nesting = belonging
- Compound suggests comfort + wellness
- Very warm imagery

**Why we passed:**
- Too domestic; limits urban appeal
- Sounds like a home goods brand
- Two syllables + two concepts = cluttered

**Verdict:** Rejected — Too domestic, not urban.

# [5] Pronunciation Guide

## The Official Pronunciation

**allura** — /ah-LOOR-ah/

### Breakdown

1. **First syllable:** "ah" — Soft, open, like the 'a' in "father"
2. **Second syllable:** "LOOR" — Emphasis here, like "lure" with rounded lips
3. **Third syllable:** "ah" — Soft finish, like a gentle exhale

### Common Mispronunciations to Correct

| Incorrect | Why It's Wrong | Gentle Correction |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| uh-LOO-rah | First syllable too closed | "Start open — think 'ah, I see'" |
| ah-LUR-ah | Second syllable too sharp | "Round your lips — 'loor' not 'lure'" |
| ah-loor-AH | Emphasis on wrong syllable | "Stress the middle — ah-LOOR-ah" |
| a-LURA | Missing first syllable softness | "Three syllables, not two" |

### Teaching Mnemonic

*"Ah, look at the aura around us."*

- **Ah** = first syllable
- **loor** (like "look" with an 'r') = second syllable
- **ah** (like "aura") = third syllable

### Audio Branding Note

When spoken aloud, allura should sound like:
- A gentle invitation
- A warm breath
- The beginning of a conversation

Encourage team members to say it slowly, warmly, and with a slight smile — the name carries the brand's emotional DNA.

# [6] Usage Rules

## The Non-Negotiables

### 1. Always Lowercase

**Correct:** allura, allura.io, the allura platform

**Incorrect:** Allura, ALLURA, The Allura Platform

**Rationale:** The lowercase form embodies our warmth and accessibility. It refuses corporate coldness. It's friendlier, more approachable, and distinctive in the tech space.

### 2. No Articles When Possible

**Preferred:** "allura helps communities connect"

**Acceptable:** "the allura platform"

**Avoid:** "The Allura"

**Rationale:** We are not a product; we are a presence. The lack of article suggests something organic, living, essential.

### 3. Spacing and Compounds

**When combining with other words:**
- allura Community (capitalized descriptor)
- allura Hub (product names capitalized)
- allura-powered (hyphenated adjective)
- Powered by allura (tagline format)

**Incorrect:** AlluraCommunity, alluracommunity, ALLURA-HUB

### 4. End-of-Sentence Placement

**Correct:** "Join the conversation on allura."

**Note:** The lowercase can confuse readers at sentence start. Avoid starting sentences with "allura" when possible. When necessary, it's acceptable:

> "allura is building something special."

### 5. Possessive Form

**Correct:** "allura's community features"

**Incorrect:** "Allura's", "ALLURA'S"

### 6. Plural and Verb Forms

**As a verb (colloquial):** "Let's allura this moment" — use sparingly, internally

**As an adjective:** "the allura experience", "allura moments"

**Never:** "alluras" (no plural form)

### 7. Logo Lockup

When the name appears with the logo:
- Maintain the wordmark's case exactly
- Don't recreate the logo in plain text
- Use approved logo files only

# [8] URL Strategy

## Domain Architecture

### Primary Domains

```
allura.io          → Main website / marketing
├── /app           → Web application (redirect to app.allura.io)
├── /about         → About page
├── /community     → Community showcase
└── /blog          → Content hub

app.allura.io      → Web application (authenticated)
├── /dashboard
├── /groups
├── /events
└── /settings

api.allura.io      → API endpoints
docs.allura.io     → Developer documentation
help.allura.io     → Support center
status.allura.io   → System status page
```

### Short Links (Marketing)

```
get.allura.io      → Main signup flow
try.allura.io      → Free trial entry
join.allura.io     → Community invitation links
share.allura.io    → Content sharing shortener
```

### Redirects

```
allura.com         → 301 → allura.io
allura.co          → 301 → allura.io
www.allura.io      → 301 → allura.io (always non-www)
```

## URL Naming Conventions

### Product Features

```
allura.io/groups        → Feature landing page
app.allura.io/groups    → Actual feature interface
allura.io/groups/[slug] → Public group profile
```

### User Profiles

```
allura.io/u/[username]  → Public user profile
app.allura.io/me        → Own profile/settings
```

### Events

```
allura.io/events/[slug] → Public event page
app.allura.io/events    → Event management interface
```

### Shareable Content

```
allura.io/s/[hash]      → Short shared link (redirects to full URL)
```

## SEO Considerations

1. **Canonical domain:** allura.io (non-www)
2. **Hreflang tags:** For future internationalization
3. **Sitemap structure:** Separate sitemaps for marketing vs. app content
4. **Robots.txt:** Block app.allura.io from indexing (authenticated content)
